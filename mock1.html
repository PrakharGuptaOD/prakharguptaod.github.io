Answer: 10

Q9. Find the Eigenvalues of the matrix C = [3, 0; 0, 5].
Answer: 3 and 5
Explanation: For a diagonal matrix, the eigenvalues are simply the diagonal entries.
Q10. If a drone wants to scale its speed in X by 2 and Y by 3, what will be the scaling matrix?

Explanation: A scaling matrix in 2D is represented as a diagonal matrix with the scaling factors on the diagonal.

  2. Product of its eigenvalues (4 x 9 = 36).

Explanation: With the eigenvector matrix assumed to be the identity (determinant 1), the final product is 1 x 36 = 36.

4. Smart Traffic Signal System using Markov Chains

Q1. A Markov Chain is called memoryless because:
Answer: B) Transition depends only on the current state
Explanation: The Markov property states that the future state depends only on the current state and not on the sequence of events that preceded it.
Q2. In the given scenario, the stationary distribution represents:
Answer: C) The long-term probability of being in each signal state
Explanation: The stationary distribution describes the steady-state probabilities for each state over time.
Q3. The sum of probabilities in any row of the transition matrix must be:
Answer: B) 1
Explanation: Each row represents a probability distribution over the next states; therefore, the sum must equal 1.
Q4. If state S4 has high stationary probability, it indicates:
Answer: B) Emergency occurs frequently
Explanation: A high stationary probability for S4 (emergency mode) implies that the system is often in the emergency state.
Q5. Which of the following is true for a regular Markov Chain?
Answer: B) There exists a stationary distribution
Explanation: A regular Markov chain always converges to a unique stationary distribution.
Q6. What is the probability of reaching S2 from S1 in exactly one step?
Answer: 0.7
Explanation: The transition probability from S1 to S2 is given as 0.7 in the transition matrix.
Q7. What is the probability of being in S3 after 2 steps starting from S1?
Answer: 0.46
Explanation: By multiplying the transition probabilities along the possible two-step paths from S1 to S3, the total probability is computed to be 0.46.
Q8. Calculate the sum of all stationary probabilities.
Answer: 1
Explanation: A stationary distribution is a probability distribution, so all its probabilities sum to 1.
Q9. If the system stays in S3, calculate the expected number of steps before moving to any other state. (Hint: Expected steps = 1 / (1 - P(S3 -> S3)))
Answer: 1.25
Explanation: Given P(S3 -> S3) is 0.2, the expected number of steps is 1 / (1 - 0.2) = 1 / 0.8 = 1.25.
Q10. If eigenvalue lambda = 1 corresponds to the stationary distribution, calculate the product of the determinant of P and the sum of all eigenvalues of P.
Answer: 0.0324
Explanation: The sum of the eigenvalues equals the trace (sum of the diagonal entries) of the transition matrix. With the computed determinant (approximately 0.036) and the trace (approximately 0.9), their product is 0.036 x 0.9 ≈ 0.0324.

Answer: c) Bubble Sort
Explanation: The term “bubble up” comes directly from Bubble Sort, where the largest element in the unsorted part of the list moves to its correct position in each pass.

Answer: b) Merge Sort
Explanation: Merge Sort divides the dataset into halves, recursively sorts each half, and then merges the sorted halves.

Answer: a) Bubble Sort; c) Insertion Sort; d) Quick Sort

MSQ Q9. In which situations is Insertion Sort efficient?
Answer: a) Small Dataset; b) Nearly Sorted Data; d) Already Sorted Data
Explanation: Insertion Sort has low overhead and performs very well on small or nearly sorted datasets.
Q10. Merge Sort is preferred over Bubble Sort because:
Answer: a) Faster on large data; c) Divide and Conquer strategy
Explanation: Merge Sort is significantly faster than Bubble Sort (O(n log n) vs. O(n²)) and uses a divide and conquer approach.

Answer: Insertion Sort
Explanation: Insertion Sort is highly efficient for small datasets due to its simplicity and low overhead.

Q1. Which of the following sorting algorithms is comparison-based?

Q2. Which sorting technique is considered the most efficient for sorting large datasets?
Answer: c) Merge Sort

Q3. What is the worst-case time complexity of Quick Sort?
Answer: a) O(n²)
Explanation: Although Quick Sort often performs at O(n log n) on average, its worst-case occurs when the pivot selection is poor, resulting in O(n²) time.

Explanation: The term “bubble up” comes directly from Bubble Sort, where the largest element in the unsorted part of the list moves to its correct position in each pass.

Answer: b) Merge Sort
Explanation: Merge Sort divides the dataset into halves, recursively sorts each half, and then merges the sorted halves.
